Q 1.7 Describe legal guidelines and constraints that impact digital projects

In 2018 the EU commission has created the General Data Protection Regulation, or GDPR, to monitor how businesses handle and process data. This act has replaced previous data protection legislations from the 1990s and was created to protect the privacy and rights of individuals. Considering the economic and social integration of markets, rapid technological advancements and the availability of public data used both by corporations and individuals both has led to a staggering increase in cross-border flows of data. GDPR requires consent to use data, and ensures a consistent level of protection for all individuals to prevent the movement of personal data within the internal market. Individuals can face prosecution for data protection breaches and contains provisions that certain disclosure of personal data is a criminal offence under UK law.
